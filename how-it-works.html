Four-phase managed process

How It Works

halfoff.ai is structured as a managed replacement program, not a staffing arrangement. The process starts with what the business actually uses and ends with an operated platform.

01

Workflow discovery

Document the screens, approvals, reports, and handoffs the team actually uses.

02

Clean-room build

Build original software from agreed requirements, not from vendor code or private materials.

03

Authorized migration

Move client-owned data through a planned sync, test, cutover, and support sequence.

04

Managed hosting

Operate the replacement with hosting, patching, break-fix support, and scoped maintenance.

Open evidence

A deep-dive on discovery, clean-room build, authorized migration, and managed hosting.

Finding

Phase 1 - Discovery

The team documents workflows, approvals, reports, data objects, roles, and operational edge cases. This produces the agreed functional scope.

Finding

Phase 2 - Clean-room build

Implementation is based on requirements and workflow behavior. Vendor source code, private materials, and copy-led reconstruction are not part of the process.

Finding

Phase 3 - Authorized migration

Data migration is planned through sync, validation, test runs, cutover, and support. The goal is continuity through process discipline.

Finding

Phase 4 - Managed hosting

After launch, the replacement is hosted, patched, monitored, and maintained under the managed contract. Net-new work is scoped separately.
