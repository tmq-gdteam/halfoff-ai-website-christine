Case Study

Engineering approach and capability

The problem

The challenge

The obvious way to build live voice AI is to chain separate models together  speech-to-text, then translation, then text-to-speech. That stitching introduces lag, and lag destroys the natural flow of a real conversation. Doing it properly requires building unified, performance-critical infrastructure.

The approach

How it was engineered

polished.cx is an original real-time voice processing layer built as a single pipeline rather than stitched parts. It was engineered to handle accent adaptation, background-noise suppression, translation, and preservation of the speaker's own voice  in-flight, during a live call.

The build

What was built

A unified, real-time speech-to-speech pipeline designed to handle accent adaptation, noise suppression, translation, and voice preservation in-flight.
